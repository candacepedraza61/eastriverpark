East Coast Resiliency Project not entirely welcome in Lower East Side.

by Candace Pedraza

December 20, 2021

The East Coast Resiliency Project is an unprecedented development underway on the East Side of Manhattan. In several parks along the East Side, construction has already begun on a flood wall or other flood prevention projects. Lower Manhattan is a specific target of this construction as flooding has been a problem for this area since Hurricane Sandy hit in 2012 and since another large storm this past September.

However, several community organizations and Councilperson-elect Christopher Marte are speaking out against plans to close East River Park until 2022 or later to get the project done. For them, it represents a broken promise from the City Council and Mayor’s office.

New York City’s Department for Design and Construction stated that the new plan brings the park itself into a “protected zone,” which is a big benefit to the community living near the East River in the face of rising water levels due to climate change.
